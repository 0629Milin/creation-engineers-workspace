Potential Transformer

A potential transformer, also called a PT, is an instrument transformer used in power systems for voltage transformation. It converts higher voltage values to lower voltage values for measurement and protection purposes. By presenting a minimal load, it maintains an accurate voltage ratio and phase relationship, ensuring precise secondary connected metering.

Like every other transformer, this transformer also consists of primary winding and secondary winding. However, the primary winding consists of more turns, whereas the secondary winding consists of a lower number of turns. The configuration of potential transformers may vary, but they function on the same principle, electromagnetic induction. For ensuring complete safety, reliability, and accurate voltage regulation, it is considered one of the best transformers to use.

Applications

  • Offers protection to electrical devices and equipment.
  • Keeps check on industrial load and voltage level.
  • Utilized in protective relay devices and metering devices.
  • Maintains synchronization between the power generation line and feeder.

Features

  • Best quality materials ensure reliability and precise voltage.
  • Available in both 1-phase and 3-phase configurations.
  • Accuracy class: 0.5, 1.0
  • Insulation class: B, F, H
  • Voltage level: up to 0.66KV
  • Burden: up to 100VA
  • Customizable to meet customers’ requirements.
