Magnet Coils

Solenoid Coils are electromechanical components that convert electrical energy into mechanical forces. By generating a magnetic field, they control the movement of valve spools, regulating the flow of fluids in various systems. These coils are highly versatile and used in devices ranging from production machinery and toys to appliances and automated equipment.

Typically used to switch applications on or off, solenoid coils activate a movable armature when voltage is applied. This armature engages with and activates the connected mechanism. Solenoid coils have diverse applications in industrial processes, including locking mechanisms and automation systems. They are often used to control hydraulic and pneumatic valves in industrial environments.

The OAC Vibratory Feeder Coil is widely used in feeding technology. It serves as the drive in systems like spiral conveyors, sorting pots, oscillating tables, and conveying channels. Unlike unbalanced drives, these vibrating solenoids immediately deliver full power when switched on without starting or stopping effects.
